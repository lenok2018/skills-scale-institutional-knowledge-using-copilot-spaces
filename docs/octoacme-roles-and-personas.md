# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## UX Designer

### Role Summary
UX Designers ensure products deliver intuitive, accessible, and delightful user experiences. They collaborate with Product Managers, Developers, and stakeholders to translate user needs and business goals into effective interface designs and interaction patterns.

### Responsibilities
- Conduct user research and usability testing to understand user needs and pain points
- Create wireframes, prototypes, and high-fidelity designs
- Develop and maintain design systems and UI component libraries
- Collaborate with developers to ensure design feasibility and quality implementation
- Validate designs through user feedback and analytics
- Advocate for accessibility and inclusive design practices

### Goals
- Improve user satisfaction and task completion rates
- Reduce user friction and support requests
- Ensure consistency across product experiences
- Balance user needs with technical constraints and business objectives

### Typical Communication
- Design reviews with Product Managers and stakeholders
- Handoff sessions with developers to clarify design specifications
- User research findings presentations and reports
- Collaboration with Technical Writers on user-facing documentation
- Design critique sessions with peer designers

---

## Technical Writer

### Role Summary
Technical Writers create and maintain clear, accurate documentation that helps users, developers, and stakeholders understand and use products effectively. They bridge technical complexity with user-friendly explanations.

### Responsibilities
- Write user guides, API documentation, and technical specifications
- Collaborate with Developers, UX Designers, and Product Managers to understand features
- Maintain documentation standards and style guides
- Review and edit technical content for clarity and accuracy
- Ensure documentation is up-to-date with product changes
- Organize information architecture for optimal discoverability

### Goals
- Reduce support burden through comprehensive, clear documentation
- Enable user self-service and developer productivity
- Maintain documentation quality and consistency
- Ensure documentation releases align with product releases

### Typical Communication
- Documentation reviews with subject matter experts (SMEs)
- Content planning sessions with Product Managers
- Coordination with UX Designers on in-product help and microcopy
- Participation in sprint planning for documentation requirements
- Stakeholder reviews for release notes and announcements

---

## QA Lead

### Role Summary
QA Leads define testing strategy, coordinate quality assurance activities, and ensure products meet quality standards before release. They lead QA teams and collaborate with engineering to build quality into the development process.

### Responsibilities
- Develop comprehensive test strategies and plans
- Lead and mentor QA team members
- Coordinate test execution across manual and automated testing
- Define quality metrics and acceptance criteria standards
- Identify and prioritize quality risks and gaps
- Champion test automation and continuous testing practices
- Coordinate cross-functional quality reviews and sign-offs

### Goals
- Prevent production defects and reduce customer-reported issues
- Increase test coverage and automation rates
- Reduce time-to-market through efficient testing processes
- Foster a culture of quality across the organization

### Typical Communication
- Test planning and risk assessment meetings with Project Managers
- Daily coordination with Developers on feature readiness and bug triage
- Quality gate reviews before releases
- Weekly quality metrics reporting to stakeholders
- Post-release retrospectives to identify quality improvements

---

## Business Analyst

### Role Summary
Business Analysts bridge business needs and technical solutions by analyzing requirements, facilitating stakeholder communication, and ensuring delivered solutions meet business objectives. They work closely with all roles to translate strategy into actionable requirements.

### Responsibilities
- Elicit, analyze, and document business requirements
- Facilitate workshops and stakeholder interviews
- Create process flows, use cases, and business requirements documents
- Validate that solutions align with business needs and ROI expectations
- Identify opportunities for process improvements and efficiency gains
- Support User Acceptance Testing (UAT) and change management

### Goals
- Ensure clear, complete requirements that minimize rework
- Maximize business value and ROI from delivered solutions
- Facilitate alignment between business stakeholders and delivery teams
- Reduce scope creep through effective requirements management

### Typical Communication
- Requirements gathering sessions with business stakeholders
- Specification reviews with Product Managers and Developers
- UAT coordination and results reporting
- Regular status updates to business sponsors
- Collaboration with Project Managers on scope and timeline impacts

## Collaboration Between Roles

Effective project delivery depends on clear collaboration and handoffs between these personas:

### Design & Development Handoffs
- **UX Designer → Developer**: Design specifications, interactive prototypes, and design system components ensure consistent implementation
- **UX Designer → Technical Writer**: Design rationale and user flows help writers create accurate, user-centered documentation

### Quality & Release Coordination
- **QA Lead → Developer**: Test results, bug reports, and quality metrics drive iterative improvements
- **QA Lead → Project Manager**: Quality status and risk assessments inform release readiness decisions

### Requirements & Planning
- **Business Analyst → Product Manager**: Business requirements and stakeholder priorities shape product roadmap decisions
- **Business Analyst → Developer**: Detailed specifications and acceptance criteria guide implementation

### Documentation & User Experience
- **Technical Writer → UX Designer**: Documentation needs and user questions inform UX improvements
- **Technical Writer → Developer**: Technical accuracy reviews ensure documentation matches implementation

### Cross-functional Alignment
- All roles participate in sprint planning, demos, and retrospectives to maintain shared context
- Project Managers coordinate handoffs and dependencies across all roles
- Regular syncs between UX Designer, QA Lead, and Business Analyst ensure quality, usability, and business value are balanced throughout delivery
